Before You Start

Understand the Funnel First

Before touching any tool, you need to understand the one system that makes free traffic actually work. It's called a funnel, and it looks like this:

Traffic Source → Squeeze Page → Email List → Follow-Up Emails → Commissions

The squeeze page is the centre of everything. Never send free traffic directly to an affiliate offer — cold visitors don't buy. You capture their email first, build trust through follow-up, then promote. That's the system.

Everything in this guide is designed to get that system working for you as quickly as possible.

Step Two · Your Email List

Set Up Your Subscriber List in SendSteed

SendSteed is LeadsLeap's built-in list manager. On the free plan, you can collect subscribers and store them — but automated follow-up sequences require Pro. What you can do for free is send manual broadcast emails to your list whenever you want. That's more than enough to get started and start building relationships.

Step Three · Your Squeeze Page

Build Your First Squeeze Page

Your squeeze page has one job: collect an email address in exchange for something valuable. LeadsLeap has a built-in page builder that makes this straightforward — no coding, no design skills needed.

  • In LeadsLeap, click "My Pages" then "Create New Page"
  • Choose a squeeze page template — pick a simple, clean one. You can customise the headline and copy later
  • Write your headline — make it specific and benefit-driven. Focus on what the subscriber will be able to do after opting in
  • Add 3–4 bullet points — short, punchy lines that tell visitors exactly what they'll get
  • Connect your SendSteed list — in the form settings, link it to the list you created in Step 2
  • Set a thank-you page — this is where subscribers go after opting in. It can be a simple message or a redirect to a resource
  • Save and publish your page — LeadsLeap gives you a live URL you can share immediately
💡 Keep it simple: The best squeeze pages have one clear offer, a headline, a few bullets, and an opt-in form. Remove anything that distracts from the opt-in. Less is almost always more.
4
Step Four · Your Follow-Up

Send Your Welcome Email — And Plan Your Follow-Up

On the free plan, emails aren't sent automatically — you send them manually as broadcasts. That sounds like extra work, but it's actually a good habit early on. It keeps you actively engaging with your list rather than setting and forgetting.

  • As soon as someone opts in, go to SendSteed → your list → Send Broadcast, and send your welcome email. Thank them, deliver what you promised, and tell them who you are in 2–3 sentences
  • A few days later, send a value email: share a tip, a quick win, or a personal story about how you got started. Build trust before you recommend anything
  • Then send a recommendation email: introduce LeadsLeap (or whatever you're promoting), explain what it is, why you use it, and include your affiliate link naturally

Aim to email your list at least once a week. It doesn't have to be long — even a short, useful message keeps you present in their inbox and builds the relationship over time.

Step Five · Your Traffic

Promote Your Squeeze Page for Free

Your funnel is live. Now it needs visitors. Here are the three best free traffic sources to start with — all of them are available inside or compatible with LeadsLeap.

  • LeadsLeap Co-op Ads: Earn credits by viewing other members' ads, then use those credits to show your squeeze page to the same community. Active marketers, real traffic — completely free
  • LeadsLeap Network Ads: Submit your squeeze page as a network ad. It shows up across the LeadsLeap ecosystem and drives steady daily visitors without any extra effort
  • Traffic Exchanges: Sites like TrafficAdBar, EasyHits4U and ListSurfing let you earn visitors by viewing other members' pages. Point your credits at your squeeze page URL — not an affiliate offer

Start with 20–30 minutes per day. View ads in LeadsLeap and any traffic exchanges you join. Use every credit earned to send traffic to your squeeze page. Consistency over a few weeks is what builds momentum.

💡 Track your links: Use LeadsLeap's built-in link tracker to create a tracked version of your squeeze page URL. This shows you exactly how many clicks each traffic source sends — so you know what's working.

Step Six · Your Daily Routine

The 30-Minute Daily Routine That Builds Your List

You don't need hours every day. What you need is consistency. Here's the routine I recommend for anyone just getting started — 30 minutes a day, every day.

  • 5 min — Check your stats: Open LeadsLeap and look at your link tracker. How many clicks did your squeeze page get? How many opt-ins? This keeps you focused on what matters
  • 10 min — Earn traffic credits: View ads in LeadsLeap and any traffic exchanges you've joined. Every credit earned is a visitor you can send to your squeeze page
  • 10 min — Promote your page: Submit your credits to show your squeeze page across the network. Check that your ads are active and running
  • 5 min — Email your list: Once a week, send a broadcast to your list — a tip, a resource, a story, or a recommendation. Staying in touch is what turns subscribers into buyers
💡 The compound effect: 30 minutes a day doesn't feel like much. But after 30 days of consistent effort, you'll have a growing list, a tested squeeze page, and real data on what's working. That's a business.
